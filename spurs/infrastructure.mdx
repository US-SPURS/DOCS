# Infrastructure & Presence

US-SPURS is designed around a hybrid infrastructure model that combines fixed facilities, regional capability centers, and mobile deployment systems.

## Headquarters Concept

US-SPURS requires a central command and planning environment capable of supporting leadership, operations, engineering, data, and cross-mission coordination.

### Headquarters Functions

- Strategic command and mission planning
- Leadership and administrative operations
- AI and data operations support
- Engineering and innovation coordination
- Interagency and partner collaboration

## Alameda Naval Air Station Alignment

A major strategic facility concept for US-SPURS is alignment with redevelopment and repurposing opportunities associated with Alameda Naval Air Station.

### Potential Roles for the Site

- West Coast command and logistics hub
- Deployment staging area
- Advanced testing and prototyping campus
- Mobile systems operations center
- Emergency response support base
- Engineering, energy, and autonomy demonstration site

## Regional Operations Centers

US-SPURS is intended to operate through regional centers that allow decentralized execution while maintaining central alignment.

### Typical Regional Center Functions

- Regional coordination and planning
- Equipment and supply staging
- Mission support and deployment prep
- Local infrastructure interface
- Technical operations support

## Mobile Deployment Units

US-SPURS places strong emphasis on deployable capability.

### Examples

- Mobile command units
- Mobile health and support units
- Communications vehicles
- Temporary power systems
- Water purification and field utility systems
- Temporary housing and stabilization kits

## Engineering & Innovation Campuses

Beyond headquarters and regional centers, US-SPURS may require dedicated engineering and innovation environments to test, build, and validate systems before field deployment.

### Possible Functions

- Systems prototyping
- Training and simulation
- Equipment integration and testing
- Resilience validation
- Operational technology demonstrations

## Partnerships

US-SPURS is designed to work across multiple sectors.

### Government Partnerships

- Federal agencies
- State and local governments
- Emergency management organizations
- Public infrastructure authorities

### Private Sector Collaboration

- Engineering and construction firms
- Technology providers
- Manufacturing and logistics partners
- Infrastructure and utility operators

### Research & Academic Partners

- Universities
- Applied research centers
- Independent labs
- Workforce and technical training partners

## Physical Deployment Philosophy

US-SPURS treats physical presence as a capability system rather than just a collection of buildings. The infrastructure footprint should enable:

- Command and coordination
- Logistics and mobility
- Technical integration
- Rapid deployment
- Long-term resilience building
