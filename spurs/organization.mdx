# Organizational Structure

US-SPURS is organized around centralized strategic leadership with distributed operational execution. The structure is designed to support both long-range planning and rapid real-world deployment.

## Executive Leadership

### Director

The Director provides agency-wide strategic direction, long-term planning, mission authorization, and top-level coordination.

**Current Director:** Philip A. Cotton Jr.

### Deputy Director

The Deputy Director supports agency-wide operational coordination and implementation of strategic priorities.

### Chief Operations Officer

Responsible for mission readiness, field coordination, deployment support, and execution oversight.

### Chief Technology Officer

Leads development of AI systems, computing infrastructure, software platforms, and cybersecurity architecture.

### Chief Infrastructure Officer

Oversees infrastructure engineering, modernization initiatives, power systems, and major physical development programs.

## Core Divisions

### 1. Emergency Response & Disaster Operations

Focuses on rapid deployment during crises, disaster relief coordination, temporary systems restoration, and stabilization support.

### 2. Infrastructure Engineering & Development

Leads critical infrastructure planning, construction, modernization, and resilient systems engineering.

### 3. Advanced Research & Technology

Develops and evaluates emerging technologies, engineering prototypes, and future operational capabilities.

### 4. Artificial Intelligence Systems

Builds and operates ATLANTIS AI, multi-agent coordination systems, predictive tools, and operational intelligence platforms.

### 5. Logistics & Supply Chain Operations

Coordinates movement of personnel, equipment, supplies, and mission resources across operational theaters.

### 6. Field Operations

Executes missions directly through deployable teams, engineering units, operational support crews, and mobile capability groups.

### 7. Public Systems & Civic Technology

Modernizes digital service delivery, public information systems, civic platforms, and technology-enabled government interaction.

### 8. Energy & Sustainability Systems

Designs and deploys renewable energy systems, microgrids, backup power, and resilience-focused energy programs.

### 9. Communications & Network Systems

Maintains secure communications, emergency network capability, field connectivity, and resilient data transport systems.

### 10. Strategic Operations

Handles complex, multi-division missions requiring high-level coordination, planning, and cross-domain execution.

### 11. Training & Operational Readiness

Prepares personnel and teams through exercises, simulations, technical training, leadership development, and readiness validation.

### 12. Administration & Support Services

Provides human resources, finance, procurement, compliance, and internal support functions across the organization.

## Operating Principles

US-SPURS is structured to function with the following principles:

- Centralized strategy with decentralized action
- Technical depth combined with operational speed
- Inter-division collaboration instead of isolated silos
- Mission-oriented execution supported by resilient systems
- Long-term capability building alongside near-term response

## Coordination Model

Most US-SPURS missions involve multiple divisions working in parallel. For example, a major disaster response may combine:

- Emergency Response & Disaster Operations
- Infrastructure Engineering & Development
- Logistics & Supply Chain Operations
- Field Operations
- Communications & Network Systems
- Artificial Intelligence Systems

This structure enables rapid mobilization without sacrificing technical coordination or command visibility.
